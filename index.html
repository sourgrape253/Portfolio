Lost Nomad

Developed with Unreal Engine & C++

Lost Nomad is a futuristic, first-person, 3D platformer. You play as the survivor of crashed transport ship and with your oxygen supply running out, you must quickly navigate through a harsh environment in order to try and find a way home.

This game was deveoped with a small team consisting of 2 artists and myself as the programmer. The game was made using a mixture of Unreal Engine's blueprinting system and C++. I was responsible for all the rogramming; including gameplay, UI/UX, and level design.


Burning of the Vanities

Developed with Unreal Engine & C++

Burning of the Vanities is a first-person, book-burning simulator. It is a proof of concept focusing on the socio-political system of fascism. As the player, you are provided with 10 books a day to either keep or destroy. Destroying the books maintains order and control. Keeping the books, allows the world to read them and become more chaotic and fight the faces of order and control.

This prototype/proof of concept was made in a few days by a team of 4 (2 artists and 2 programmers). I was responsible for the gameplay and physics using Unreal Engine Blueprints and C++.


Sudoku

Developed with LOVE2D using Lua

A simple sudoku puzzle solver. I made this using the LOVE2D game engine. It was also my first project on the engine and using Lua as a language. Essentially it will solve any sudoku you enter into it. You can also randomly generate your own puzzles with varying difficulty to test your skills.


Physics Birds

Developed with OpenGL using C++

This was a game I made using OpenGL. It runs 3 levels simulating the game Angry Birds. You can shoot 3 birds at the pigs and the blocks and they all have simulated physics. It was my first experience working with physics outside of big engine like Unreal or Unity, and thus, quite the learning curve.


Cloth Simulator

Developed with OpenGL using C++

This was an experiment with simulated cloth physics using OpenGL and C++. You can grow the colth, use various shapes to interact with it, cut holes in it, blow wind on it, and drag it around the scene. I made this after the angry birds project (and playing a lot of the Batman games).
